About Me

Overcoming financial challenges, I have pursued academic growth and research opportunities. I am an undergraduate in Mechanical Engineering (Gujarat Technological University, CGPA 8.61/10) with strong experience in OpenFOAM and ANSYS Fluent. My research interests include Computational Fluid Dynamics, Heat Transfer, Multiphase Flow, and integrating Machine Learning with CFD. I completed internships at FOSSEE (IIT Bombay) and IIT Patna where I worked on film cooling, vortex shedding, multiphase modeling, and HPC workflows.

Projects

Design of Ocean Wave Energy Harvesting System Using Piezoelectric

Supervisors: Dr. Lav Kaushik, Dr. Liril Silvi — Research Grant ₹80,000

Performed vibration-induced simulations coupled with a piezoelectric model in Ansys Mechanical. Responsible for system modelling, dynamic analysis and documentation. Demonstrated innovation in wave-to-electricity conversion and secured seed funding.

Numerical Analysis of Air Film Cooling (OpenFOAM)

FOSSEE, IIT Bombay — OpenFOAM v2312

Reproduced and validated film-cooling results from literature (Li & Wang) using OpenFOAM. Implemented a 2D flat plate case using blockMesh, used buoyantBoussinesqSimpleFoam for an incompressible conceptual study, and performed post-processing in ParaView and MATLAB. Investigated effects of blowing ratio, mainstream temperature, and injection angle on adiabatic film cooling effectiveness.

OpenFOAM v2312 ParaView Project Link

Effect of Hole Geometry on Film Cooling Effectiveness

Compressible flow simulations using OpenFOAM — k-ε turbulence model

Conducted simulations to study how coolant hole geometry (cylindrical vs fan-shaped) affects film cooling performance at subsonic conditions. Validated numerical results against experimental data and identified fan-shaped holes improve film cooling effectiveness.
